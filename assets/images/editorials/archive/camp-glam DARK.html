Camp Glam
Sequins, survival energy, and a campfire that never quite goes out. A love letter to people who pack both hiking boots and eyeliner.
Camp Glam started as a conversation and turned into a small universe: Tina Grimm — model, outdoor obsessive, last woman standing on season two of Netflix's Outlast — wanted to collide ultra-glam styling with the kind of campsite you probably shouldn't wander into alone at night.

We leaned into headlamps, lanterns, and cheap string lights as set dressing, letting the desert do what it does best: turn everything cinematic the second the sun disappears.
Model Tina Grimm
Concept Camp Glam — dreamed up by Tina, built together on location.
Location Mojave Desert, California
Photography Ric Berryman
Shot on Digital & 35mm film — a little grit left on purpose.

Built before Outlast, this shoot was never about survival TV — it was about that feeling of being a little too far from the city, wrapped in sequins and smoke, waiting for the next log on the fire.
